Stage 1
The Seed
Dry · Dormant · Waiting
Speed:
You are touching a seed — a tiny package of life. Inside the hard outer coat called the seed coat, there lives a baby plant called the embryo, and stored food called the endosperm. The seed is dry and resting, waiting for water, warmth, and air to begin growing. This resting state is called dormancy. Seeds can stay dormant for many years!
Hard outer coat is called the seed coat
Baby plant inside is called the embryo
Seeds need water, warmth & air to grow
Resting state is called dormancy
💧
Stage 2
Water Absorption
Imbibition · Awakening
Speed:
The seed has absorbed water! This process is called imbibition. Water enters the seed, making it swell. The seed coat softens and splits open. Water activates special proteins called enzymes that break down stored food into energy. The seed is waking up! The first tiny root, called the radicle, pushes downward into the soil to anchor the plant and absorb more water.
Water absorption is called imbibition
Seed coat softens and splits open
Enzymes activate to release energy
First root is called the radicle
🌱
Stage 3
Sprouting
Germination · First Leaves
Speed:
The seed is sprouting — this is called germination! Feel the curved stem called the hypocotyl pushing upward through the soil. At the top are two thick seed leaves called cotyledons, bent like a hook to push through soil without damage. They carry the stored food. Below ground, roots branch out to collect water and minerals. The plant still uses its stored food because it cannot yet make food from sunlight.
This process is called germination
Curved stem is called the hypocotyl
Seed leaves are called cotyledons
Roots branch to absorb water & minerals
🌿
Stage 4
Young Seedling
Photosynthesis · Growing
Speed:
You are touching a young plant called a seedling! Feel the tall straight stem. At the top are the first true leaves containing chlorophyll — a special substance that captures sunlight. The plant now makes its own food through photosynthesis: it takes in carbon dioxide from air and water from soil, and uses sunlight to make sugar. The roots below have grown deep and wide to support the plant. One day it will produce its own seeds!
Young plant is called a seedling
Leaves contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis
Makes food from sunlight, water & CO₂
Roots grow deep to support the plant
